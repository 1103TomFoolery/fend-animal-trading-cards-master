Red-Tail Hawk

The red-tailed hawk is a popular bird in falconry, particularly in the United States where the sport is tightly regulated.

  • Diet: Carnivorous - small mammals, birds and reptiles
  • Vocalization: Two to three second rasping scream that begins at a high pitch and slurs downward
  • Flight: When soaring or flapping typically travels approximately 40 mph and up to 120 mph when diving
  • Habitat: Mixed forrest and field, with high bluffs or trees.
  • Distribution: Widespread throughout North America.

The red-tailed hawk is a bird of prey, one of three species colloquially known in the United States as the "chickenhawk," though it rarely preys on standard sized chickens. Wikipedia Link
